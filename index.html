Body doubling for the neurodivergent brain

Presence is a neurodiversity-affirming skill-building companion — rooted in evidence-based research, inspired by lived experience & guided by spiritual wisdom. For anyone whose brain, body, or nervous system makes daily participation feel harder than it should. ✨

Free to start · No account required · Built by an ADHD OT, for the neurospicy community

How it works

As simple as
sitting together

Body doubling is one of the most effective and underused neurodivergent tools. The presence of another person — even a quiet, ambient one — regulates the nervous system and anchors attention.

Step 01

🎯

Name your task

Say it out loud — or type it. Naming activates your working memory and signals to your brain: we're doing this now.

Step 02

🫂

Your companion arrives

Your Presence companion is here — calm, non-judgmental, and just... there. No performance needed. No shame spiral invited.

Step 03

⏱️

Work in gentle cycles

Focus in nervous-system-friendly time blocks — shorter, flexible, and kind. Break when you need. Return when you can.

Step 04

✨

Celebrate what happened

Every session ends with acknowledgement — not just what you finished, but that you showed up at all. That counts.

🧠

Why body doubling works: the neuroscience

Research suggests that for neurodivergent people, executive function is not absent — it is context-dependent. The presence of another person activates social motivation pathways and the dopaminergic reward system, making task initiation and sustained attention significantly easier. Occupational therapists have used body doubling as an evidence-informed strategy for decades.

— Drawing on research by Dr. Russell Barkley, the ADHD-OT framework, and monotropism theory (Murray, Lesser & Lawson, 2005)

Task initiation difficulties in ADHD are partly about the brain not being able to "see" the path to completion. Breaking tasks into visible micro-steps makes the invisible path suddenly navigable.

Gifts & Blessings of the Neurospicy 🧠

Your brain isn't broken.
It's different by design.

A neurodiversity-affirming space to explore the extraordinary qualities that come alongside a neurodivergent mind — grounded in research, lived experience, and occupational therapy perspectives.

🌀 ⭐ Deep dive

The Attention Tunnel: Monotropism & the Gift of Going Deep

When your interest catches fire, something extraordinary happens. The neurodivergent brain doesn't just pay attention — it dives. Monotropism, the tendency to channel attention into a narrow beam of intense focus, explains why neurodivergent people can code for 9 hours straight but forget to eat, or write a novel in a weekend but lose their keys. This isn't a flaw. It's a feature of a brain that found something worth all of itself.

Grounded in: Monotropism theory · Murray, Lesser & Lawson (2005) · Hyperfocus & dopamine regulation

🌀
☁️ Creativity

The Creative Daydream: Why Your Wandering Mind is Actually Working

Default Mode Network (DMN) activity — the brain's "daydreaming" state — is elevated and differently regulated in ADHD. What looks like zoning out is often a surge of associative, divergent thinking that underlies creative insight.

Grounded in: Default Mode Network research · ADHD & divergent thinking

⚡ Inspiration

Sparks of Inspiration: The Brilliant, Chaotic Gift of Idea Storms

neurodivergent brains show heightened sensitivity to novelty and reward. That's why the first moment of a new idea can feel electric — almost unbearable in its excitement. This isn't impulsivity. It's a low threshold for wonder.

Grounded in: Dopamine & novelty-seeking · Interest-based nervous system

💛 Emotional depth

Feeling Everything: Emotional Intensity as a Superpower

Rejection-sensitive dysphoria and emotional intensity are real — and painful. But they come packaged with empathy so deep it can feel like telepathy, and joy so full it fills a whole room. sensitive hearts feel loudly, and that's a gift worth holding carefully.

Grounded in: Rejection Sensitive Dysphoria (Dr. Dodson) · Emotional intelligence & ADHD

⏳ Time & presence

Living in "Now": The ADHD Relationship with Time as Deep Presence

Time blindness isn't laziness — it's a neurological difference in prospective memory and time perception. But inside that "now-time" lives a radical presence, a total immersion in this moment that most people spend years trying to cultivate in meditation.

Grounded in: Dr. Barkley's time-blindness model · Mindfulness & ADHD

🔭 Pattern thinking

Connecting the Invisible Dots: ADHD & Intuitive Pattern Recognition

Many neurodivergent people describe "just knowing" things — connections between seemingly unrelated ideas that others miss entirely. This is often fast, parallel, intuitive processing: a cognitive style that can feel chaotic from inside but produces genuine breakthroughs.

Grounded in: Cognitive style research · Systems thinking in ADHD

🚀 Performance

Crisis Genius: Why ADHD Brains Thrive When Stakes Are High

Deadline-induced dopamine is real. When the pressure rises and the timeline collapses, many neurodivergent people discover an uncanny ability to hyperfocus, problem-solve rapidly, and deliver. Understanding this pattern — rather than exploiting it — can reshape how you structure your whole work life.

Grounded in: Urgency & ADHD performance · External accountability structures

🌈 Sensory world

A World in High Definition: Sensory Richness & Aesthetic Sensitivity

When every sensation arrives at full volume, life is vivid in ways that can overwhelm — but also astound. Many ADHD individuals have heightened aesthetic sensitivity, noticing beauty, texture, sound, and light with an intensity that fuels artistry and deep connection to the physical world.

Grounded in: Sensory processing in ADHD · OT sensory integration theory

🔗 ⭐ Autism · Deep dive

The Gift of Deep Connection: How Autistic Love & Loyalty Run Differently — and Deeper

Autistic love is often all-in. When an autistic person cares — about a person, a cause, a field of knowledge — they care with their whole being. No half-hearted interest, no performative connection. This depth of loyalty and authentic relating is one of autism's most profound and underappreciated gifts.

Grounded in: Autistic relational styles · attachment & authenticity research · double empathy problem

🔗
🎯 Autism · Focus

Monotropism: The Superpower Hidden Inside "Obsession"

Especially prominent in autism, monotropism is the tendency to channel attention into one burning interest with complete immersion. What others call obsession, monotropic minds experience as total, joyful, expertise-building devotion. Many of history's greatest specialists were monotropic.

Grounded in: Monotropism theory · Murray, Lesser & Lawson (2005) · autistic cognition

🧩 Autism · Systems thinking

The Systems Brain: Seeing What Everyone Else Missed

Autistic thinkers often naturally see the whole system — the rules, the patterns, how all the parts connect — in ways neurotypical minds miss. This is why autistic people so often spot the inconsistency, the flaw in the plan, or the deeper truth that everyone else glossed over.

Grounded in: Systemizing theory · Baron-Cohen · autistic cognitive strengths research

🌿 Autism · Authenticity

Radical Honesty: The Gift of Meaning What You Say

Many autistic people carry a deep discomfort with dishonesty and social performance. What gets labeled as "bluntness" is actually a profound commitment to truth — a refusal to play social games that aren't real. In a world full of masks, this is a radical and beautiful gift.

Grounded in: Autistic communication styles · double empathy problem · Milton (2012)

🌍 Autism · Justice

The Justice Compass: Fierce Advocacy for What's Right

A heightened sense of justice and fairness is one of the most consistent traits across the autistic experience. Many autistic people feel injustice viscerally — and that sensitivity fuels some of the most powerful advocates, activists, and ethical thinkers our world has ever seen.

Grounded in: Autistic values & moral cognition · neurodiversity advocacy movement

💬 Autism · Empathy

Your Empathy Was Never Missing: The Double Empathy Problem

For decades, autism was described as a deficit in empathy. Research now shows something far more nuanced: autistic people often have profound empathy — sometimes overwhelming — but express and process it differently. The gap is bidirectional. Autistic people understand each other with remarkable ease.

Grounded in: Double empathy problem · Dr. Damian Milton (2012) · autistic-to-autistic connection research

🔬 Autism · Expertise

The Deep Diver: How Special Interests Build World-Class Knowledge

Special interests are not a quirk to manage — they are a superpower to cultivate. The depth of knowledge autistic people develop around their passions routinely surpasses what any curriculum produces. History's greatest scientists, composers, and mathematicians show this pattern everywhere you look.

Grounded in: Special interests & expertise development · autistic strengths-based approaches

🌱 Autism · Sensory

Sensory Poetry: When the World Arrives in Full Resolution

Autistic sensory experience can be overwhelming — and transcendent. The same nervous system that struggles with fluorescent lights can experience music as physical bliss, or notice beauty in textures, colors, and sounds that most people filter out entirely. Life in high definition.

Grounded in: Autistic sensory processing · hyper/hyposensitivity research · OT sensory integration

🎨 Autism · Creativity

Outside the Box Isn't a Place — It's Home

When you've never quite fit the neurotypical template, you develop an extraordinary relationship with originality. Autistic creativity emerges from genuinely different ways of perceiving and expressing experience — art, music, writing, and innovation that could only come from a brain wired this way.

Grounded in: Autistic creativity research · divergent thinking & perceptual differences

🌀 AuDHD · Both worlds

AuDHD: The Beautiful Contradiction of Being Both

The monotropic focus of autism meeting the novelty-seeking of ADHD. The need for routine colliding with the craving for spontaneity. Many AuDHD people describe their experience not as double the difficulty — but double the depth, double the creativity, double the magic.

Grounded in: AuDHD co-occurrence research · overlapping neurotype profiles · lived experience

💎 All neurotypes · Resilience

The Resilience Forged in Difference

There is a particular kind of resilience that grows in people who have always had to work harder to navigate a world not designed for them. Not the resilience of toughness — the resilience of creativity, adaptability, and the profound self-knowledge that comes from a lifetime of paying close attention to yourself.

Grounded in: Neurodivergent resilience · post-traumatic growth · strengths-based OT practice
